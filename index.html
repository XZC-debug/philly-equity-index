Methodology

Data Sources

  • Census Tracts 2010 (OpenDataPhilly)
  • OPA Properties — 462,744 residential parcels
  • Crime Incidents 2023+ — 161,976 records
  • Schools (495), Hospitals (36)
  • Transit Stops (20,238), Recreation (171)
  • ACS 2023 5-Year — income, poverty, demographics

Composite Score Formula

All components min-max normalized to [0,1] before weighting.

I = 0.20·Income_norm + 0.20·(1 − Crime_norm) + 0.15·(1 − School_dist_norm) + 0.15·(1 − Hospital_dist_norm) + 0.15·Transit_norm + 0.10·Property_value_norm + 0.05·Recreation_norm

Final score scaled to 0–100. Quintile ranks assigned via Jenks natural breaks.

Spatial Autocorrelation

Global Moran's I = 0.735 (z = 29.83, p < 0.001) confirms strong spatial clustering — livability scores are not randomly distributed across the city.

Local Moran's I (LISA) classifies each tract as HH (hot spot), LL (cold spot), HL/LH (spatial outliers), or NS (not significant at p < 0.05).

Bivariate Classification

Tracts classified into a 3×3 grid by tercile rank of livability score vs. pct_nonwhite. The "Double Disadvantage" cell (low score + high non-white %) captures 114 tracts — the primary Environmental Justice finding.

Priority Intervention Zones

Operational definition: livability score ≤ 40th percentile AND pct_nonwhite ≥ 60th percentile. Identifies 144 tracts where resource deprivation overlaps with racial minority concentration.

Average household income within PIZ: $41,654 vs. $75,440 citywide average outside PIZ — a $33,786 gap.
